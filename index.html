About Me


I grew up in the small town of Manson WA. and when I say small I mean it. with a population of less than 2000. I loved the security of living there, but soon enough life would lead me in a very different direction. At the age of 18 I volunteered for a two year mission trip with my church and they needed me in New York City. Contrary to my own expectations, I grew to enjoy living in the big city. However at the end of two years I returned to washington. It was time to get an education and move forward with my life.

It wasn't easy for me to decide on a career. I spent over a year trying to get my feet wet in different fields from mechanical engineering, to Music, then Sound Engineering. All interested me but I still felt like I hadn't found my niche. Then one day while working at my minimum wage job an idea struck. It was an idea for an app. and the more I thought about it the idea evolved and became more real. I began to research hour after hour until I came to a conclusion. I either needed to learn to code, or I needed a lot of money to make my idea a reality. I've always enjoyed school, and learning and picking up new skills. But for me coding was special. It was a whole new world I had never even step foot in. I knew I needed to become a programmer.

In my hometown there were not too many opportunities so I decided to pack my bags and move back to New York City. Within days of moving I came in contact with a school that was offering a nine month full stack web development Coding Bootcamp, and within a month I was enrolled. I loved every step of the way. Every day a new challenge would present itself, and I had to be the one to solve it. I think back to when my professor told me at the beginning of the course, that sometimes he would get so into coding he would forget to eat lunch. I didn't realize that if I didn't make an effort to set alarms for breaks, I would become that type of programmer as well. I was hooked.

I've spent the last several years working as a software developer at a small tech startup adding to my skillset gaining experience. From working with the company ERP and CMS, to migrating the codebase from python 2 to 3, and designing the new user interface in Vue.js, I've continued to learn new skills and become more confident in my abilities.
